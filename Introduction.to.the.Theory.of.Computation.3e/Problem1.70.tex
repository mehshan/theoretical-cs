\begin{problem}{1.70}
We define the \textbf{avoids} operation for languages $A$ and $B$ to be
\[
A \; avoids \; B = \{w \; | \; w \in A \; and \; w \; doesnâ€™t \; contain \; any \; string \; in \; B \; as \; a \; substring\}.
\]
Prove that the class of regular languages is closed under the \textit{avoids} operation.
\end{problem}

\begin{idea}
If both $A$ and $B$ are regular languages, then we can build a regular expression that describes $A \ avoids \ B$ as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Construct a language $C$ that consists of all strings that contain strings of $B$ as substring.
\item Take complement of $C$ to construct a new language $\overline{C}$ that consists of all strings that contains all strings that do not contain strings of $B$ as substring.
\item Take intersection of $A$ and $\overline{C}$ to construct $A \ avoids \ B$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{idea}

\begin{proof}
To prove that the class of regular languages is closed under the \textit{avoids} operation we use the closure of the class of regular languages under intersection and complement. The regular expression $A \cap \overline{(\Sigma^* \cdot B \cdot \Sigma^*)}$ describes the language $A \ avoids \ B$.
\end{proof}
