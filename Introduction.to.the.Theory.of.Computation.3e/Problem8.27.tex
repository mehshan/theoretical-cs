\begin{problem}{8.27}
Recall that a directed graph is strongly connected if every two nodes are connected by a directed path in each direction. Let
\[
STRONGLY\text{-}CONNECTED = \{\langle G \rangle \ | \ G \text{ is a strongly connected graph}\}.
\]
Show that $STRONGLY\text{-}CONNECTED$ is NL-complete.
\end{problem}

\begin{proof}
To show that $STRONGLY\text{-}CONNECTED$ is NL-complete, we must show that it is in NL and that all NL problems are log space reducible to it.

% Part 1
% Show that co-STROnGLY-CONNECTED is NL
% Non-deterministically select two nodes
% Call NTM for coPATH on the two nodes
% If NTP accepts, then accept

To prove that $STRONGLY\text{-}CONNECTED$ is NL-hard, we give a log space reduction from $PATH$ to $STRONGLY\text{-}CONNECTED$. This reduction converts a directed graph $G$ and two nodes $s$ and $t$ to a directed graph $G'$ so that $G$ has directed path from $s$ to $t$, iff $G'$ is strongly connected.

\end{proof}
